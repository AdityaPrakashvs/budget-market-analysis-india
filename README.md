# 📊 Budget vs Stock Market Analysis (India)

📈 Equity Research | 📊 Financial Data Analysis | 🇮🇳 India Markets

---

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes the impact of Union Budget announcements on major Indian stock market indices — **BSE Sensex** and **NSE Nifty** — over the period **2010 to 2024**.

The study focuses on understanding how fiscal policy announcements influence market movements, volatility, and investor sentiment.

---

## 🎯 Objective
- To examine the correlation between Union Budget announcements and stock market performance  
- To analyze market volatility around budget periods  
- To understand investor behavior and sentiment during major economic events  

---

## 🧠 Methodology

The analysis combines both **quantitative and qualitative approaches**:

### 📊 Quantitative Analysis
- Correlation Analysis (using Excel CORREL function)  
- Regression Analysis to study trends over time  
- Year-wise comparison of market reactions  

### 📚 Qualitative Analysis
- Literature review of previous research  
- Study of investor sentiment and behavioral factors  

---

## 📂 Dataset & Files

- 📄 **Research Paper:**  
  *Correlation of Union Budget of India and Stock Market Indices (2010–2024)*  

- 📊 **Excel File:**  
  Contains historical data of BSE Sensex and NSE Nifty along with correlation calculations  

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

- 📉 Market reactions to budget announcements are **highly volatile**  
- 🔄 Both **positive and negative correlations** observed across years  
- 📊 Correlation values fluctuate significantly, indicating **inconsistent market behavior**  
- 📉 Regression analysis shows a **declining trend**, suggesting reduced impact of budget over time  
- 🧠 **Investor sentiment plays a major role** in influencing market reactions

## 📈 Insights

- Stock markets are not driven only by policy announcements  
- External factors like:
  - Global economic conditions  
  - Political stability  
  - Investor psychology  
  also significantly influence market behavior  

---

## ⚠️ Limitations

- Correlation does not imply causation  
- External macroeconomic factors may influence results  
- Limited to historical data (2010–2024)  

---

## 🛠 Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel  
- Statistical Analysis (Correlation & Regression)  

---

## 📚 Conclusion

The study concludes that while Union Budget announcements do impact stock markets, the relationship is **complex, inconsistent, and influenced by multiple factors**. Over time, the direct impact of budget announcements appears to be **weakening**, indicating increasing market maturity.
